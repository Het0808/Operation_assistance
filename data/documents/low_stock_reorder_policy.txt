PURPOSE
Define the triggers, responsibilities, and lead times for replenishing inventory before stockout occurs.

REORDER TRIGGERS
Each product in the catalog has a defined Reorder Point (ROP) stored in the inventory system.
When available stock (on-hand minus reserved) drops to or below the ROP, a reorder alert is raised
automatically by the inventory system.

Default ROP formula (used when a custom ROP is not set):
  ROP = (Average Daily Sales × Lead Time in Days) + Safety Stock
  Default Safety Stock = 5 units for standard items, 10 units for high-velocity items.

LEAD TIMES BY SUPPLIER
- Supplier A (domestic): 5–7 business days.
- Supplier B (electronics): 10–14 business days.
- Supplier C (overseas, bulk): 21–28 business days.
Lead times should be reviewed each quarter and updated in the supplier master file.

APPROVAL THRESHOLDS
- Reorders under $500 total value: Inventory Manager approves autonomously.
- Reorders $500–$2,000: Requires countersignature from Operations Manager.
- Reorders over $2,000: Requires sign-off from Finance before purchase order is raised.

EMERGENCY STOCK PROCEDURE
If a stockout occurs on a high-demand item, the Inventory Manager may place an emergency order
directly with any available supplier at market price, up to $800, without waiting for standard approval.
Emergency orders must be documented in the incident log within 24 hours.

OVERSTOCK POLICY
Items with more than 180 days of cover (stock on hand ÷ average daily sales) are flagged for review.
Options: promotional discount, bundle with related product, or return to supplier if contract allows.
