DOCUMENT: Product Damage Claims Procedure
CATEGORY: Policy / Procedure
LAST UPDATED: 2025-10-30
OWNER: Customer Support Lead

---

PURPOSE
Provide a clear, consistent process for handling claims where a product arrives damaged,
is defective out of the box, or is lost in transit.

STEP 1 — CUSTOMER REPORTS DAMAGE
Customer contacts support@opsco.internal within 72 hours of confirmed delivery.
Subject line must include the order ID (format: ORD-XXXXX).
Customer must attach:
  (a) At least two photographs of the damage.
  (b) A photograph of the outer packaging.
  (c) The delivery note or confirmation email.

STEP 2 — INITIAL ASSESSMENT (within 1 business day)
Support agent reviews the claim and categorises it:
  - Category A: Clear carrier damage (packaging crushed, item broken). Approve replacement immediately.
  - Category B: Possible misuse or ambiguous damage. Escalate to Inventory Manager for inspection request.
  - Category C: Item works but has cosmetic damage only. Offer a 15% partial refund or keep-and-replace.

STEP 3 — RESOLUTION OPTIONS
For approved Category A claims:
  - Replacement shipped within 2 business days at no cost.
  - Customer does not need to return the damaged item unless unit value > $300.

For Category B claims under investigation:
  - A prepaid return label is issued. Item must be received at the warehouse within 10 business days.
  - If confirmed defective: full replacement or refund at customer's choice.
  - If found to be customer-caused damage: claim denied; customer notified with photo evidence.

For Category C (cosmetic only):
  - Partial refund processed within 3 business days, or replacement dispatched on customer's preference.

STEP 4 — CARRIER CLAIM
For Category A damage, the support team files a parallel claim with the carrier.
Carrier claim reference number must be added to the order notes.
Carrier typically resolves within 14–21 business days; reimbursement credited to operations budget.

ESCALATION
If a customer disputes a denied claim, the issue is escalated to the Operations Manager.
Final decisions at that level are binding.
