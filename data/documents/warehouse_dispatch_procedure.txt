PURPOSE
Standardise the pick, pack, and dispatch workflow to ensure accurate and timely shipments.

DAILY SCHEDULE
06:00 — Warehouse opens; staff briefing and shift allocation.
07:00 — Order queue downloaded from the order management system (OMS).
07:30 — Picking begins. Priority order: Expedited > Standard > Bulk freight.
12:00 — Midday cut-off for same-day expedited dispatch.
14:00 — Final standard packing complete.
15:00 — Carrier collection (FastFreight Logistics van arrives).
16:30 — End-of-day inventory count for high-velocity items.
17:00 — Warehouse closes.

PICKING PROCESS
1. Picker receives a digital pick list on the handheld scanner.
2. Each item is scanned at the shelf location and again at the packing station.
3. A mismatch alert (wrong SKU or quantity) halts the pick and triggers a supervisor check.
4. Back-ordered items are flagged in the OMS and the customer is notified by email automatically.

PACKING STANDARDS
- Use the smallest box that fits the item with at least 5 cm padding on each side.
- Fragile items: double-box and mark outer packaging FRAGILE.
- Batteries: comply with carrier dangerous goods policy (form DG-3 must accompany shipment).
- Each box must contain: item(s), packing slip, and returns label.

DISPATCH RECORDS
Every dispatched order is recorded in the OMS with:
  - Dispatch timestamp
  - Carrier name and tracking number
  - Staff member ID who completed packing

ERRORS AND MISPICKS
A mispick discovered after dispatch must be reported to the supervisor within 30 minutes.
A replacement order is raised immediately and a prepaid return label is sent to the customer.
Mispick incidents are logged monthly; three mispicks in a month by the same staff member triggers retraining.
